Trust After Everything Falls Apart

Exodus Field Notes - May 28, 2026

In stable societies, trust is something we rarely think about. We trust that food will be available, that contracts will be honored, and that strangers will generally follow basic social rules. After collapse, none of these assumptions hold. Every interaction becomes a calculation of risk. The Exodus series examines this shift with unusual clarity. Characters must decide who is worth risking everything for, often with very little information. Small betrayals carry enormous consequences. The books show how communities form around shared necessity rather than shared values, and how fragile those bonds remain. This is one of the most realistic elements of the series. Trust is not a feeling. It is a repeated pattern of behavior that must be proven again and again. The stories explore what happens when that pattern breaks and how difficult it is to repair.
